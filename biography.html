Welcome to the biography of Sunita Williams

Sunita Willims was born on Sept 19 1965.
She is a retired US navy captian and former NASA astronaut

Early life

Sunita Lyn Pandya Williams was born on September 19, 1965, in Euclid, Ohio, and grew up in Needham, Massachusetts.
Her father, Deepak Pandya, was an Indian neuroanatomist, and her mother, Ursuline Bonnie Pandya, was a Slovene American.
She graduated from Needham High School in 1983 and later earned a Bachelor of Science from the United States Naval Academy in 1987. She also received a Master of Science in Engineering Management from the Florida Institute of Technology in 1995.

Military career

1. Naval Career

Sunita Williams was commissioned as an ensign in the United States Navy in 1987 and became a naval aviator in 1989.
She served with Helicopter Combat Support Squadron 8 and took part in overseas deployments during Operation Desert Shield and Operation Provide Comfort, as well as Hurricane Andrew relief operations.
During her naval career, she flew more than 3,000 hours in over 30 different aircraft types.

2. Test Pilot & NASA Selection

Williams trained at the U.S. Naval Test Pilot School and later worked as a test pilot and instructor, flying several types of helicopters and aircraft.
She was later assigned to USS Saipan as Aircraft Handler and Assistant Air Boss.
In 1998, while deployed on Saipan, she was selected by NASA for the astronaut program.

Career in NASA

Williams began her astronaut candidate training at the Johnson Space Center in August 1998

STS-116 Mission

Sunita Williams was launched to the International Space Station on December 9, 2006, aboard Space Shuttle Discovery as part of the STS-116 mission.
She joined the Expedition 14 crew and later continued with Expedition 15 after the Russian crew members were rotated in April 2007.

Expeditions 14 and 15

Spacewalks and Records

Sunita Williams completed three spacewalks in early 2007 with astronaut Michael López-Alegría.
By the end of these missions, she had spent 29 hours and 17 minutes in spacewalks, setting a record for the most spacewalk time by a woman at that time.

Marathon in Space

On April 16, 2007, Williams became the first person to run a marathon in space while aboard the International Space Station.

Return to Earth

Williams returned to Earth on June 22, 2007, aboard the STS-117 mission after spending 192 days in space. 🚀
